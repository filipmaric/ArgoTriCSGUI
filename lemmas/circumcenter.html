Circumcenter

Proposition: All three side bisectors \(b_a\), \(b_b\), and \(b_c\) meet in a single point (circumcenter \(O\))
Proposition:
  • Side bisector \(b_a\) contains midpoint \(M_a\) of segment \(BC\) and is perpendicular to line \(a\).
  • Side bisector \(b_b\) contains midpoint \(M_b\) of segment \(AC\) and is perpendicular to line \(b\).
  • Side bisector \(b_c\) contains midpoint \(M_c\) of segment \(AB\) and is perpendicular to line \(c\).
Proposition: Circumcircle \(o\) is the circle centered at the circumcenter \(O\) containing vertices \(A\), \(B\), and \(C\).
