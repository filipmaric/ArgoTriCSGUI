Orthocenter

Proposition: All three altitudes \(h_a\), \(h_b\), and \(h_c\) meet in a single point (orthocenter \(H\)).
Proposition:
  • Altitude foot \(H_a\) is the intersection of altitude \(h_a\) and side \(a\).
  • Altitude foot \(H_b\) is the intersection of altitude \(h_b\) and side \(b\).
  • Altitude foot \(H_c\) is the intersection of altitude \(h_c\) and side \(c\).
Proposition:
  • Altitude \(h_a\) contains vertex \(A\) and is perpendicular to side \(a\).
  • Altitude \(h_b\) contains vertex \(B\) and is perpendicular to side \(b\).
  • Altitude \(h_c\) contains vertex \(C\) and is perpendicular to side \(c\).
