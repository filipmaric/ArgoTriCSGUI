Euler points

Proposition:
  • Euler point \(E_a\) is the midpoint of segment \(HA\), connecting vertex \(A\) and orthocenter \(H\).
  • Euler point \(E_b\) is the midpoint of segment \(HB\), connecting vertex \(B\) and orthocenter \(H\).
  • Euler point \(E_c\) is the midpoint of segment \(HC\), connecting vertex \(C\) and orthocenter \(H\).
Proposition:
  • Euler point \(E_a\) belongs to altitude \(h_a\).
  • Euler point \(E_b\) belongs to altitude \(h_b\).
  • Euler point \(E_c\) belongs to altitude \(h_c\).
Proposition:
  • Euler line \(e\) is the line that contains circumcenter \(O\), centroid \(G\), center of Euler (nine point) circle \(N\) and orthocenter \(H\).
  • Centroid \(G\) divides segment \(HO\) where \(H\) is the orthocenter and \(O\) is the circumcenter in ratio 2:1 i.e. \(HG:GO = 2:1\)
Proposition: Euler (nine point) circle \(n\) is the circle that contains side midpoints \(M_a\), \(M_b\), \(M_c\), altitude feet \(H_a\), \(H_b\), \(H_c\) and Euler points \(E_a\), \(E_b\) and \(E_c\).
Proposition:
  • Center \(N\) of Euler (nine-point) circle \(n\) is the midpoint between the orthocenter \(H\) and circumcenter \(O\).
  • Centroid \(G\) divides the segment \(NO\) where \(N\) is the center of Euler (nine-point) circle and \(O\) is the circumcenter in ratio 1:3, i.e. \(NG:NO = 1:3\).
  • Center of Euler (nine-point) circle \(N\) divides the segment \(HG\) where \(H\) is the orthocenter and \(G\) is the centroid in ratio 3:4, i.e. \(HN:HG = 3:4\).
Proposition:
  • Circle \(c_{EaA}\) centered at Euler point \(E_a\) containing the vertex \(A\) also contains the orthocenter \(H\) and altitude feet \(H_b\), and \(H_c\).
  • Circle \(c_{EbB}\) centered at Euler point \(E_b\) containing the vertex \(B\) also contains the orthocenter \(H\) and altitude feet \(H_a\), and \(H_c\).
  • Circle \(c_{EcC}\) centered at Euler point \(E_c\) containing the vertex \(C\) also contains the orthocenter \(H\) and altitude feet \(H_a\), and \(H_b\).
