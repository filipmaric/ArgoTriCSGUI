Angle bisector feet

Proposition:
  • Foot \(T_a\) of angle bisector \(t_a\) is its intersection with the side \(a\).
  • Foot \(T_b\) of angle bisector \(t_b\) is its intersection with the side \(b\).
  • Foot \(T_c\) of angle bisector \(t_c\) is its intersection with the side \(c\).
Proposition:
  • Distance from angle foot \(T_a\) to side \(b\) is equal to distance from \(T_a\) to side \(c\), i.e., segments \(T_aT_{ab}\) and \(T_aT_{ac}\) are congruent (they join \(T_a\) and its perpendicular projections \(T_{ab}\) and \(T_{ac}\)).
  • Distance from angle foot \(T_b\) to side \(a\) is equal to distance from \(T_b\) to side \(c\), i.e., segments \(T_bT_{ba}\) and \(T_bT_{bc}\) are congruent (they join \(T_b\) and its perpendicular projections \(T_{ba}\) and \(T_{bc}\)).
  • Distance from angle foot \(T_c\) to side \(a\) is equal to distance from \(T_c\) to side \(b\), i.e., segments \(T_cT_{ca}\) and \(T_cT_{cb}\) are congruent (they join \(T_c\) and its perpendicular projections \(T_{ca}\) and \(T_{cb}\)).
Proposition:
  • Sides b and c are tangent to the circle \(c_{Ta}\) centered at angle bisector foot \(T_a\) that contains projections \(T_{ab}\) and \(T_{ac}\) of the point \(T_a\) onto sides \(b\) and \(c\).
  • Sides a and c are tangent to the circle \(c_{Tb}\) centered at angle bisector foot \(T_b\) that contains projections \(T_{ba}\) and \(T_{bc}\) of the point \(T_b\) onto sides \(a\) and \(c\).
  • Sides a and b are tangent to the circle \(c_{Tc}\) centered at angle bisector foot \(T_c\) that contains projections \(T_{ca}\) and \(T_{cb}\) of the point \(T_c\) onto sides \(a\) and \(b\).
