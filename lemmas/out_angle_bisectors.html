External angle bisectors

Proposition:
  • Two external angle bisectors \(s_b\) and \(s_c\) and internal angle bisector \(t_a\) meet at single point \(I_a\) that is the center of excircle \(i_a\).
  • Two external angle bisectors \(s_a\) and \(s_c\) and internal angle bisector \(t_b\) meet at single point \(I_b\) that is the center of excircle \(i_b\).
  • Two external angle bisectors \(s_a\) and \(s_b\) and internal angle bisector \(t_c\) meet at single point \(I_c\) that is the center of excircle \(i_c\).
Proposition:
  • The midpoint \(M_a\) of segment \(BC\) is also a midpoint of the segment \(S'_aT'_a\), where \(T'_a\) is the foot of the projection of incenter \(I\) onto line \(a\), and \(S'_a\) is the foot of the projection of outer incenter \(I_a\) onto \(a\).
  • The midpoint \(M_b\) of segment \(AC\) is also a midpoint of the segment \(S'_bT'_b\), where \(T'_b\) is the foot of the projection of incenter \(I\) onto line \(b\), and \(S'_b\) is the foot of the projection of outer incenter \(I_b\) onto \(b\).
  • The midpoint \(M_c\) of segment \(AB\) is also a midpoint of the segment \(S'_cT'_c\), where \(T'_c\) is the foot of the projection of incenter \(I\) onto line \(c\), and \(S'_c\) is the foot of the projection of outer incenter \(I_c\) onto \(c\).
Proposition:
  • Line that joins incenter \(I\) and midpoint \(M_a\) of segment \(BC\) is parallel to the line that joins vertex \(A\) and foot \(S'_a\) of the projection of outer incenter \(I_a\) onto \(a\).
  • Line that joins incenter \(I\) and midpoint \(M_b\) of segment \(AC\) is parallel to the line that joins vertex \(B\) and foot \(S'_b\) of the projection of outer incenter \(I_b\) onto \(b\).
  • Line that joins incenter \(I\) and midpoint \(M_c\) of segment \(AB\) is parallel to the line that joins vertex \(C\) and foot \(S'_c\) of the projection of outer incenter \(I_c\) onto \(c\).
Proposition:
  • Vertex \(A\) lies on the circle over segment \(T_aS_a\), where \(T_a\) is the foot of angle bisector \(t_a\) (i.e., its intersection with line \(a\)), and \(S_a\) is the foot of outer angle bisector \(s_a\) (i.e., its intersection with line \(a\)).
  • Vertex \(B\) lies on the circle over segment \(T_bS_b\), where \(T_b\) is the foot of angle bisector \(t_b\) (i.e., its intersection with line \(b\)), and \(S_b\) is the foot of outer angle bisector \(s_b\) (i.e., its intersection with line \(b\)).
  • Vertex \(C\) lies on the circle over segment \(T_cS_c\), where \(T_c\) is the foot of angle bisector \(t_c\) (i.e., its intersection with line \(c\)), and \(S_c\) is the foot of outer angle bisector \(s_c\) (i.e., its intersection with line \(c\)).
