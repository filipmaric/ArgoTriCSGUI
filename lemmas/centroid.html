Centroid

Proposition: All three medians \(m_a\), \(m_b\), and \(m_c\) meet in a single point (centroid \(G\))
Proposition:
  • \(M_a\) is the midpoint of segment \(BC\).
  • \(M_b\) is the midpoint of segment \(AC\).
  • \(M_c\) is the midpoint of segment \(AB\).
Proposition:
  • Median \(m_a\) joins vertex \(A\) and midpoint \(M_a\) of segment \(BC\).
  • Median \(m_b\) joins vertex \(B\) and midpoint \(M_b\) of segment \(AC\).
  • Median \(m_c\) joins vertex \(C\) and midpoint \(M_c\) of segment \(AB\).
Proposition:
  • Centroid \(G\) divides median \(AM_a\) in 2:3 ratio (\(AG\): \(AM_a\) = 2:3).
  • Centroid \(G\) divides median \(BM_b\) in 2:3 ratio (\(BG\): \(BM_b\) = 2:3).
  • Centroid \(G\) divides median \(CM_c\) in 2:3 ratio (\(CG\): \(CM_c\) = 2:3).
Proposition:
  • Line \(M_bM_c\) is paralel to side \(a\)
  • Line \(M_aM_c\) is paralel to side \(b\)
  • Line \(M_aM_b\) is paralel to side \(c\)
